Explanation

Invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC) is the most common type of breast cancer. In simple terms, it started in the milk ducts (DCIS) and has now grown slightly outside of the ducts (IDC).

The tumor is small‚Äîless than a centimeter, or about the size of a dime. The initial tests show no signs of spread to the lymph nodes, which is often a positive sign for successful outcome for women who have a similar cancer profile.

The Nottingham Grade is a grading system used to describe how much the cancer cells look like normal cells and how quickly they are growing. Grade 2 is considered intermediate‚Äînot the slowest, but also not the fastest or most aggressive.

Your cancer is hormone receptor positive, which means it is fueled by hormones. You can think of these hormones as "food" for the cancer cells. This means the primary, highly effective long-term treatment will be anti-hormone pills (endocrine therapy) to "starve" the cancer.

Patients with HER2 negative status do not overproduce the HER2 protein and typically do not need the specific, targeted medications designed for HER2-positive cancer.
