Cancer Type
Invasive Ductal Carcinoma

Invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC) is the most common type of breast cancer. In simple terms, it started in the milk ducts (DCIS) and has now grown slightly outside of the ducts (IDC).

Location
Left breast (8mm)

The tumor is smallâ€”less than a centimeter, or about the size of a dime. The initial tests show no signs of spread to the lymph nodes, which is often a positive sign for successful outcome for women who have a similar cancer profile.

Stage
Clinical stage I/II
Grade
Nottingham grade 2

The Nottingham Grade is a grading system used to describe how much the cancer cells look like normal cells and how quickly they are growing. Grade 2 is considered intermediateâ€”not the slowest, but also not the fastest or most aggressive.

Hormone Receptor Status
Estrogen positive, Progesterone positive

Your cancer is hormone receptor positive, which means it is fueled by hormones. You can think of these hormones as "food" for the cancer cells. This means the primary, highly effective long-term treatment will be anti-hormone pills (endocrine therapy) to "starve" the cancer.

HER2 Status
HER2 negative

Patients with HER2 negative status do not overproduce the HER2 protein and typically do not need the specific, targeted medications designed for HER2-positive cancer.
